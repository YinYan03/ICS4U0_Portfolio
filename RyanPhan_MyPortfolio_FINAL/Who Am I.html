Who Am I?

Based on the results of two personality tests I have taken, it seems that I am a “Guardian” and “ESFJ”. These tests can be found at https://profile.keirsey.com/#/b2c/assessment/start and http://www.humanmetrics.com/cgi-win/jtypes2.asp. According to the Myers Briggs personality criteria, ESFJ places my personality type as an extrovert, I tend to perceive things using my senses, I weigh decisions using my feelings and I prefer to be organized and structured. According the personality test, being a “Guardian” generally means that I am a practical person who abides by the rules and can cooperate with others.

With regards to my potential career paths, I seem well suited to leadership-style jobs, as I tend to take on the role of the practical leader in the workplace. I work best under circumstances where things are clear, predictable, and structured. In addition, I tend to find satisfaction in my job when I am able to help others and that my work is appreciated by others. Thus, jobs with human interaction and feedback tend to suit me well. Potential jobs include those in the medical sector and social work. Teaching is also a suitable role for people with my personality type. A role as an administrator also suits my personality type. Essentially, any role within important social institutions such as hospitals or schools, where there is also a structured and organized layout suits me.

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of ESFJs, it seems as if I would learn best in group based situations, where the expectations of the group are clearly outlined. One skill mentioned in my results was the skill of connecting with others. I learn best by talking to others about certain concepts and ideas, as it gives me an opportunity to learn from others as well as gaining new perspectives on certain ideas. As ESFJs learn best in group-based situations, it is fair to assume that I my learning style is a fairly even mix of visual, auditory, and kinesthetic styles, as all three of these learning styles are employed in group based situations.

For my personal evaluation on my results, I personally believe that my results are partially accurate. Overall, most of the skills and traits that “Guardians” and ESFJs possess suit me, with a few outliers. Two major negative outliers that I noticed were the handling of criticism and opinions on change. According to one of the websites, ESFJs are “vulnerable to criticism”, which I find is only partially true for me. While I admit that at times I can become defensive, in the end I usually end up listening to and accepting the criticism of others, as opposed to being hurt in reaction to criticism. As for opinions on change, “Guardians” are stated to be “not very comfortable winging it or blazing new trails”, which I find is untrue for myself, as I often find myself in situations where I need to think on my feet and improvise, as there are times when plans end up not going as planned. One positive outlier that I sometimes seem to lack is sensitivity, as sometimes I accidentally end up offending or hurting others since I tend to be quite blunt at times. All in all, I did find that the rest of the traits that “Guardians” and ESFJs suit me to a certain extent.
